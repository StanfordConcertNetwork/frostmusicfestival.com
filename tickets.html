The Stanford Concert Network is excited to invite you to the 6th Annual Frost Music & Arts Festival.

Each year, we celebrate the arts by dedicating a day in the sun to admiring art created by our fellow classmates. We are given the opportunity to showcase over 40 different student commissioned art installations as well as invite a student artist to share the stage with Grammy winning performers. Thousand of student come out to support their peers, making the festival the one of the most attended events by both the undergraduate and graduate students.

Historically, the festival has been held in the bucolic Frost Amphitheater. In the warm spring air, doors open and attendees wander around the grounds, laden with student artwork and decorations. People begin filling the grassy bowl to listen to the student opener. By late afternoon, the crowd fills the grassy bowl, swaying to the music of many genres. Previous headliners include Fetty Wap, Flume, Dispatch, MGMT, and Modest Mouse.

This year, Frost Amphitheater is under renovations. We will be hosting this yearâ€™s Frost Music & Arts Festival in the Stanford Stadium. Although relocating such a large event has presented its challenges, we have also had many new opportunities. We are trying to maintain the amphitheater aesthetic, inviting guests to be on the field and up through the bleachers. For the first time, the festival will begin in the late afternoon and end after dark. The additional element of night has allowed us to experiment with different set production, creating a new and unique experience.

We hope you are excited as we are for Frost and hope to see you there on May 20th!
